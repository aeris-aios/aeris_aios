TOP HEADER / DOCK DESIGN REQUIREMENT (EXTREMELY IMPORTANT)

Recreate the top header area so it matches the reference image composition very closely.

This top section must NOT look like:
- a separate floating pill
- a dock pasted on top of the frame
- a disconnected navbar

Instead, it must look like:
- one continuous sculpted top header surface
- a seamless extension of the outer frame
- a molded header where the center dock dips downward naturally from the top edge

DESIGN BEHAVIOR

The top header should span across the upper edge of the main outer frame.

At the center of this top edge, create a dock shape that extends downward from the header itself, as if the header has been carved or molded into a recessed curved docking area.

This means:
- the left and right portions of the header continue straight across the top
- the center section curves downward into a rounded dock pocket
- the dock is visually part of the same surface as the header
- the transition between the header and dock must be seamless

VISUAL GOAL

The result should feel like:
- a single premium sculpted surface
- the dock is integrated into the frame
- the center dip is part of the architecture
- the form is smooth, refined, and intentional

It should NOT feel like:
- two separate layers
- a pill sitting on top
- a floating menu element with random spacing above it

SHAPE REQUIREMENTS

Build the top header so that:
- the main frame has a top edge/header band
- the center of that top edge contains a smooth downward extension
- this extension forms the app icon dock area
- the dock pocket has rounded lower curves and blends directly into the header

Think of it like:
- a soft notch
- a molded dock recess
- a sculpted center tray extending from the top bar

The silhouette should feel very close to the reference:
- flat across the left
- flat across the right
- dipped downward in the center
- fully symmetrical
- soft large-radius curves

DOCK CONTENT

Inside that integrated dock area:
- place a row of small colorful rounded icons
- keep them evenly spaced
- keep them centered
- do not oversized them
- the icons should sit naturally inside the dock recess

SURFACE STYLE

Because this UI is neumorphic:
- the top header and dock must share the same material
- same surface color
- same soft highlights
- same depth language
- same sculpted feel

Use subtle neumorphic shaping so the center dock feels like part of the same soft body.

TECHNICAL IMPLEMENTATION DIRECTION

Implement the header and dock as one unified shape, not two unrelated divs.

Recommended approach:
- create one parent top frame/header container
- use either border-radius shaping, pseudo-elements, masking, clip-path, or layered curved containers to sculpt the center downward dock area
- make sure the seam between header and dock is invisible
- preserve symmetry and smooth curves

IMPORTANT

The top dock must extend BELOW the top header section seamlessly.
It should feel fused to the header, as if it naturally grows out of the frame.
This is one of the most important visual identity elements of the interface.

Do not simplify this into a standard navbar plus floating pill.
It must feel custom-designed and architectural.
